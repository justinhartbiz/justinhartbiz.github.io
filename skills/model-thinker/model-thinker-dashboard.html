üìä Models by Category

üìà Distribution Models ‚Äî How outcomes are distributed across a population
Power Law Distribution
Few winners dominate; long tail of small performers. 80/20 rule on steroids.
üí° "If top X% drives Y% of results, you're in a 'home run' game, not an 'average improvement' game."
Normal Distribution
Bell curve ‚Äî most items cluster around average, few extremes.
üí° "If normally distributed, improving the average matters more than chasing outliers."
üï∏Ô∏è Network Models ‚Äî How connections affect dynamics
Diffusion & Contagion
How information, behaviors, or patterns spread through connected networks.
üí° "If R > 1, the pattern will spread exponentially until saturation."
Network Centrality
Identifying key nodes ‚Äî who/what is most connected or influential.
üí° "Node X has disproportionate influence due to position Y."
üîÑ Dynamic Models ‚Äî How systems change over time
Markov Chains
State transitions where future depends only on current state.
üí° "Given current state X, there's P% probability of moving to state Y."
Tipping Points
Small changes accumulate until a threshold triggers dramatic change.
üí° "System is stable until threshold T is crossed, then rapid phase change."
Path Dependence
History matters; early choices constrain future options (lock-in).
üí° "Current state exists because of historical choice, not because it's optimal."
üéÆ Strategic Models ‚Äî How interactions between agents play out
Game Theory
Strategic interaction where outcomes depend on others' choices.
üí° "Given opponent strategy X, our best response is Y."
Signaling
Actions communicate information; costly signals are credible.
üí° "A $X ask signals donor capacity/commitment level Y."
Colonel Blotto
Resource allocation across multiple battlefields; can't win everywhere.
üí° "Optimal strategy concentrates resources on X fronts, concedes Y."
üß† Learning Models ‚Äî How agents adapt and improve
Multi-Armed Bandit
Balancing exploration (trying new things) vs exploitation (using what works).
üí° "Optimal strategy balances exploring unknowns vs exploiting known winners."
Rugged Landscapes
Multiple local peaks; incremental optimization may miss global optimum.
üí° "We're on peak X but peak Y may be higher ‚Äî need big jumps to find it."

üéØ How to Use Model Thinker

1

Classify Your Problem

Is this about Prediction, Explanation, Design, Decision, or Strategy?

2

Pick 3-5 Relevant Models

Ask: "What model might apply here?" Use the Quick Reference table for common scenarios. More models = more perspectives.

3

Apply Each Model's Key Question

Each model has a "key question" it helps answer. Run your data through that lens. What do you see?

4

Synthesize the Ensemble

Where do models agree? = High confidence insight
Where do they disagree? = Interesting tension or tradeoff to explore
What's the composite action? = Your recommendation
