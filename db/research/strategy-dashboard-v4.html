Research-Backed Findings (RCT Evidence)

üéØ

"Now or Never" Effect

"Extending a donation deadline from 1 week to 1 month did NOT increase participation. Almost all gifts came immediately after the ask."

Damgaard & Gravert 2017 (RCT)
DB Application:
Create urgency through content, not timeline. Assume a one-minute window to convert. Follow up within hours, not days.
üí∞

Named Match = 39% Lift

"Telling donors their gift would be matched by the Gates Foundation increased donation likelihood by 39% and gift size by 44% vs anonymous match."

Karlan & List via ideas42 2023
DB Application:
Always name the matcher. "A generous board member will match" > "Your gift will be doubled"
üö´

"Thank You in Advance" Backfires

"Anticipatory gratitude phrases trigger psychological reactance and DECREASE donations."

Bruttel et al. 2025 (RCT)
DB Application:
BAN this phrase from all scripts. Audit templates. Thank AFTER the gift.
‚ö†Ô∏è

Out-Group Framing Halves Donations

"An explicit out-group appeal halved the donation rate vs a generic ask. In-group appeals showed no significant increase over generic."

Linos et al. 2021 (APSR RCT)
DB Application:
Use broad, universal narratives in cold SMS. Avoid divisive identity framing.
üíö

Impact Receipts = 70.3% Retention

"SMS detailing exactly where a donation was used increased 12-month retention to 70.3% (vs 62.6% control)."

Chell et al. 2018 (RCT)
DB Application:
Send "impact receipt" 30 days post-donation: "Your $25 fed a family in Dallas"
üìñ

"How" Beats "Why"

"For immediate compliance, instructional 'How to help' messaging significantly outperforms abstract 'Why we need help' messaging."

Buttenheim et al. 2024 (RCT)
DB Application:
Focus deadline copy on how to donate rather than why we need you.
