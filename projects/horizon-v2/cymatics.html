What is Cymatics?

Cymatics is the study of visible sound and vibration, a scientific phenomenon where sound waves generate geometric patterns in physical matter. The term was coined by Swiss scientist Hans Jenny in 1967, but the foundations were laid by Ernst Chladni in the 18th century.

Historical Timeline

1787 - Ernst Chladni publishes "Entdeckungen über die Theorie des Klanges" (Discoveries in the Theory of Sound)

1960s - Hans Jenny conducts extensive research using electronic equipment

1967 - The term "Cymatics" is coined from the Greek word "kyma" (wave)

2000s - Modern researchers use advanced technology to further explore cymatics

How It Works

When sound vibrations are applied to a medium like sand, salt, or water:

    1. The vibrations create standing wave patterns

    2. Areas with high amplitude (antinodes) push particles away

    3. Areas with low amplitude (nodes) collect particles

    4. The result is intricate geometric patterns that directly correspond to the sound frequency

Beyond Physics

Understanding Chladni Plates

A traditional Chladni plate experiment involves:

1. A thin metal plate secured in the center

2. Sand or salt sprinkled evenly across the surface

3. A violin bow drawn across the edge of the plate

4. The resulting vibration creates node lines where the particles collect

Higher frequencies produce more complex patterns with additional nodes and antinodes, revealing the mathematical relationship between sound and form.
