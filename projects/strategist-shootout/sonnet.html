Quick Wins (Weeks 1-30)

Test 1: Evening Send Time Optimization

Hypothesis: Sending fundraising SMS between 8-9pm will increase conversion by at least 30% compared to daytime sends.

Control: Standard daytime send (1pm)

Variant: 8:30pm send time

Sample Size: 10,000 per variant (20,000 total)

Success Metric: Conversion rate Average gift

Stopping Rule: Run for 2 complete sends or until 95% confidence is reached

Expected Lift: 37% increase in conversion rate

Research Backing: MissionWired/Tatango 2024 finding that evening sends (8-9pm) have 37% higher conversion

Test 2: Named Matcher Impact

Hypothesis: Including a specific, named matching donor will increase conversion by 35%+ compared to generic "gift matched" language.

Control: "Your gift will be matched dollar-for-dollar!"

Variant: "Board member Sarah Johnson will personally match your gift dollar-for-dollar!"

Sample Size: 10,000 per variant

Success Metric: Conversion rate Average gift

Stopping Rule: Run for 2 complete sends or until 95% confidence is reached

Expected Lift: 39% in donation likelihood, 44% in gift size

Research Backing: Karlan & List via ideas42 2023

Test 3: Eliminating "Thank You in Advance"

Hypothesis: Removing "thank you in advance" phrasing and replacing with forward-looking gratitude will increase conversion.

Control: "Thank you in advance for your generous support."

Variant: "Your support today will make an incredible difference."

Sample Size: 10,000 per variant

Success Metric: Conversion rate

Stopping Rule: Run for 2 complete sends or until 95% confidence is reached

Expected Lift: 15% increase in conversion

Research Backing: Bruttel et al. 2025 finding that anticipatory gratitude phrases trigger psychological reactance

Test 4: First-Person vs. Organizational Voice

Hypothesis: First-person messages from an identified individual will outperform organizational voice by 30%+.

Control: "[Organization] needs your help to provide meals to families in need..."

Variant: "Hi, this is Sarah from [Organization]. I'm reaching out because we need your help to provide meals..."

Sample Size: 10,000 per variant

Success Metric: Click-through rate Conversion rate

Stopping Rule: Run for 2 complete sends or until 95% confidence is reached

Expected Lift: 30% increase in conversion

Research Backing: DB Creative Insights showing 72% of top-tier creatives use personalized voice

Intermediate Wins (Weeks 30-60)

Test 5: Impact Receipt Implementation

Hypothesis: Sending a specific impact update 30 days post-donation will increase 90-day retention and second gift likelihood.

Control: Standard thank you message only

Variant: Standard thank you + impact update at 30 days ("Your $25 provided 10 meals to families in Dallas")

Sample Size: All donors from prior month (split 50/50)

Success Metric: 90-day donor retention Second gift rate

Stopping Rule: Run for full 90-day cycle

Expected Lift: 12% increase in retention (70.3% vs 62.6%)

Research Backing: Chell et al. 2018 finding on impact receipts increasing retention

Test 6: List Segmentation by Engagement

Hypothesis: Sending to a more targeted, engaged segment will double conversion rates compared to full-file sends.

Control: Standard full-file send (to all 250K)

Variant: Targeted send to most engaged 50K subscribers (opened/clicked in last 60 days)

Sample Size: 50,000 (variant) vs 50,000 random from full file (control)

Success Metric: Conversion rate Revenue per message

Stopping Rule: Run for 2 complete sends or until 95% confidence is reached

Expected Lift: 87% increase in conversion (0.114% vs 0.061%)

Research Backing: MissionWired/Tatango 2024 finding that smaller lists convert better

Test 7: SMS vs. MMS for Fundraising

Hypothesis: MMS messages with relevant imagery will increase conversion but may increase opt-outs.

Control: Text-only SMS fundraising appeal

Variant: Same copy with added image (MMS)

Sample Size: 20,000 per variant

Success Metric: Conversion rate Opt-out rate Net revenue per message

Stopping Rule: Run for 3 complete sends or until 95% confidence is reached

Expected Lift: 41% higher conversion for MMS, but with higher opt-outs

Research Backing: MissionWired/Tatango 2024 benchmark showing MMS has 41% higher conversion but 70% higher opt-outs

Test 8: Universal vs. Specific Beneficiary

Hypothesis: Broad, universal appeals will outperform specific beneficiary stories in the SMS channel.

Control: "Help Maria, a single mother in Chicago, feed her family this month."

Variant: "Help families in our community put food on their tables this month."

Sample Size: 15,000 per variant

Success Metric: Conversion rate

Stopping Rule: Run for 2 complete sends or until 95% confidence is reached

Expected Lift: 25% increase for universal framing

Research Backing: Linos et al. 2021 finding that out-group framing halves donation rate

Strategic Recommendations

Insights and action items based on research synthesis

What To STOP Immediately

  1. STOP using "Thank You in Advance" phrasing

    This common copywriting phrase triggers psychological reactance and actively decreases donation rates. Remove it from all existing templates and future messages. Source: Bruttel et al. 2025

  2. STOP sending full-file blasts with generic messaging

    Data clearly shows that smaller, targeted lists convert nearly 2x better than mass blasts to larger lists. Implement basic segmentation immediately to see quick lift. Source: MissionWired/Tatango 2024

  3. STOP using generic matching language

    Replace all instances of "Your gift will be doubled" with specific, named matching sources. Research shows this simple change drives a 39% increase in donation likelihood. Source: Karlan & List via ideas42 2023

  4. STOP sending SMS during working hours

    Daytime sends (especially mid-day) underperform dramatically. Evening sends (8-9pm) have 37% higher conversion and lower opt-out rates. Source: MissionWired/Tatango 2024

  5. STOP using organizational voice in text messages

    The data clearly shows that first-person messages from an identified individual dramatically outperform organizational voice, especially in the SMS medium where personal connection is key. Source: DB Creative Insights

Highest-ROI Change To Make Tomorrow

Switch to 8-9pm Evening Send Time

The single highest-ROI change that can be implemented immediately with zero cost is shifting all fundraising SMS sends to the 8-9pm window.

Why It Works
  • Subscribers are more receptive after work hours
  • Decreased competition from other messages
  • People have more time to read and consider giving
  • Lower opt-out rates during evening hours
  • Many donors browse phones during "relaxation" time
Implementation
  • Change default send time in SMS platform
  • Queue all fundraising messages for 8:30pm local time
  • Consider time zone segmentation for national lists
  • Maintain this timing even for urgent deadline day messages
  • Reserve daytime sends for non-fundraising communications
Expected Impact

Based on MissionWired/Tatango 2024 benchmark data, this change alone should increase conversion by 37% with absolutely zero implementation cost or technical complexity.

Where Conventional Wisdom Gets It Wrong

Myth: "Frequency Drives Response"

The research clearly shows that timing matters more than frequency. Sending at optimal times (evening, deadline day) has a much greater impact than sending more messages.

Source: Damgaard & Gravert 2017

Myth: "Longer Deadlines Help"

Contrary to popular belief, extending a donation deadline from 1 week to 1 month does NOT increase the proportion of people who donate. Almost all gifts come immediately after the ask.

Source: Damgaard & Gravert 2017

Myth: "Thank You In Advance Is Polite"

This commonly used phrase actually triggers psychological reactance and decreases donations. It creates a sense of obligation that donors resist.

Source: Bruttel et al. 2025

Myth: "Specific Beneficiary Stories Always Win"

While this is true in other channels, in SMS, explicit out-group appeals actually halve donation rates compared to generic asks. Broad, universal narratives work better in the limited bandwidth of a text.

Source: Linos et al. 2021

Myth: "More Media = Better Engagement"

While MMS does have higher conversion rates, it also drives 70% higher opt-out rates than plain SMS. The benefit must be weighed against the increased list churn.

Source: MissionWired/Tatango 2024

Myth: "Post-Gift Messaging Doesn't Matter"

Sending specific impact updates 30 days post-donation increases 12-month retention to 70.3% (vs 62.6% control). Post-gift stewardship via SMS dramatically improves donor lifetime value.

Source: Chell et al. 2018
