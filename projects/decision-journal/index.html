What Makes a Good Decision Journal Entry?

Clear Context

Document the situation that requires a decision, including constraints and timing considerations.

Full Options Set

List all alternatives considered, not just the chosen path, to avoid hindsight filtering.

Explicit Assumptions

Identify what must be true for your decision to succeed, creating clear review criteria.

Risk Assessment

Acknowledge potential failure modes and document planned mitigations.

Success Metrics

Define in advance how you'll know if the decision was successful, with specific measures.

Emotional Context

Record your confidence and emotional state to correlate with outcomes over time.
