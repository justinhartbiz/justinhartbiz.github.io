The Panpsychist Answer

Panpsychism is the view that consciousness is a fundamental feature of the universe ‚Äî as fundamental as mass, charge, or spin. It's not something that emerges from complex brains; it's something that underlies all matter.

Under this view, every particle has some degree of proto-experience. A rock isn't conscious, but it has something} ‚Äî a rudimentary form of "psychological" properties that, when arranged in the right way (like a brain), give rise to full-blown consciousness.

The philosopher Philip Goff puts it this way: "Consciousness is the gold standard of what it is to be real." If you're skeptical, ask yourself: what's more certain than your own experience?

Why Take Panpsychism Seriously?

It's Not Circular

Materialism doesn't explain consciousness ‚Äî it assumes it. Every attempt to reduce experience to physical processes just describes the mechanics without explaining the "what it's like."

It's the Simplest Explanation

If consciousness is fundamental, we don't need to explain how it arises from non-conscious matter. Instead, we just need to explain how complex consciousness arises from simple consciousness.

It Solves the Combination Problem

How do billions of neurons combine to create a single unified consciousness? If each neuron already has its own proto-experience, combining them is less mysterious.

It's Scientifically Productive

Panpsychism generates testable predictions about the relationship between consciousness and quantum mechanics, pointing toward new research programs.

"If panpsychism is true, then the universe has been conscious all along ‚Äî for 13.8 billion years. The stars aren't just burning; they're experiencing something. The Combination Problem

The biggest objection to panpsychism is the "combination problem": how do billions of micro-conscious particles combine to form a single unified experience?

This is the challenge: your brain has roughly 86 billion neurons. If each has some degree of consciousness, how do they become you}?
