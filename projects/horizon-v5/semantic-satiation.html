Semantic Satiation

Say the word "moon" 50 times in a row. Somewhere around word 20, it stops sounding like a word. The sounds detach from the meaning. You're left holding a phoneme.

The Mechanism

Rapid, repeated activation of a word's neural pattern causes reactive inhibition — the neurons that encode the word's meaning fatigue, while the neurons encoding its sound continue firing. Described by Leon James (1960s) as "temporary satiation of the neural activity that arouses the semantic encoding."

The Reframe

Semantic satiation proves that language is not stored as a simple lookup table. Words are patterns of activation in neural tissue — and activation has limits. When a word loses its meaning, you're watching neurons tire in real time. Meaning is not in the word. It's in the firing.
